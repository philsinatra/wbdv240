Assignments:

Build First Pages

Due: Midnight the night before next class

Create a minimum of four html pages to be part of your final project (an online portfolio). Using HTML tags, populate as much content as possible on all of the pages.

Suggested pages:

  • about
  • portfolio
  • resume
  • index (home page)

Example: content on your 'about' page could include -Your current program of study at Drexel, why you chose your field of study, how far along you are in the program. What makes you unique? What do you like to do? What are your hobbies? A brief explanation of your past experience with web design (before this class), including HTML, XHTML, CSS, graphics, etc. (There is no need to impress me, just be honest.) If you have used any other web technologies or web development packages, include that also.

Make sure you are using your html tags properly and that they are properly nested. Do not worry about visual design, focus on adding content and HTML syntax/nesting

Site Map

Due: Midnight the night before next class

Create the first draft of your Site Map (textbook page 461). To build a Site Map, you need to know what is going to appear on your site, so spend time brainstorming. If you were going on a job interview and your instructor was your potential employeer, what would you want to show them in your portfolio? Consider building a brainstorm chart, showing what makes you different. Ask yourself the 5 Whys, http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5_Whys.

Once you have your brainstorming chart, build your Site Map. You can use paper and a pencil, or any software (Photoshop, Illustrator etc). If you draw on paper, scan your image so you can submit it online.

Final images should be:

  • 150dpi
  • 8.5 x 11
  • greyscale
  • .jpg file format

You can have as many images as necessary. If you have more than one zip all files into one zip file. Make sure to rotate your images so they are easy to read, and enhance the levels/contrast if necessary.

File names for your images should be in this format: xxx_sitemap_01.jpg (replace xx with your Drexel ID, update file numbers as needed)

Purchase/Setup Domain/Hosting

Due: Midnight the night before next class

A requirement for this class is to have your own domain and hosting plan. Prior to week 2, you must have your domain and hosting established. Bring ALL hosting information (usually a few emails) to class so we can review accessing and uploading to your server.

Review Domain Names & Hosting (textbook pages 487-488) prior to purchasing your domain and hosting plans.

Suggested providers (see who has a better deal, who is offering a discount for students, shop around):

  • Bluehost
  • Dreamhost

If you have questions or problems email your instructor as earlier as possible. You must submit a link to your new domain as part of your homework.

How to turn in homework

This week there are 4 homework assignments. You will be responsible to turn in one email, and bring one item to class.

  • Build First Pages
  • Site Map
  • Domain/Hosting

For these three assignments, you will submit one email. The email will include two zip files and a link.

The first zip file will contain all of your 'First Pages'. Place all of your .html files in a single folder, and zip that folder. If you follow the naming conventions covered in class, your folder should be called public_html. The only items in this folder should be your first pages.

The second zip file will contain all of your Site Map files. Place all of your .jpg files in a single folder, and zip that folder. Your individual files should be named in this format: xxx_sitemap_01.jpg. Your folder should be named xxx_sitemap_images.

If you can not submit both attachments in one email, separate each into it's own email and be clear that I should expect two emails for your assignment.

Your single email should now have two zip files attached. You must also include a link to your domain, for example: Domain: http://mynameis.com

Send your email to me (ps42@drexel.edu prior to 11:59 pm, the night before our next class. Assignments turned in late will receive no credit and will not be reviewed.

Regarding your reading assignment, bring with you to class a single sheet of paper. This single piece of paper will have on it three questions from the reading assignment, the answer to each question and the page number the information came from. At the top of the page you will put your name and email address. These questions will be used next class for an extra credit game. If you do not meet the list requirements, you will not be eligible to participate and will not receive credit for the assignment.

Build Site Navigation

Due: Midnight the night before next class

Add/update content on all of your pages using valid HTML tags.

Create a simple set of links so your site can easily be navigated. Focus on HTML markup and functionality, not visual design. Every page of your site should be accessible from every other page.

Since you now have a working domain and hosting, you have to upload your files to your remote server, and provide a link to your project which is now online. Submit one link to your index page, from there your instructor will be able to navigate to all of your other pages using your simple navigation links.

Build Wireframes

Due: Midnight the night before next class

Revisit your site map and make any additions/edits that are needed.

Build your first version of your Wireframes (text book page 463).

You should have a unique wireframe design for each of your web pages, even if the intended layout is similar or the same. Remember wireframes are simple sketches of how information will be laid out. What is the hierarchy?

You can use paper and a pencil, or any software (Photoshop, Illustrator etc). If you draw on paper, scan your image so you can submit it online.

Final images should be:

  • 150dpi
  • 8.5 x 11
  • greyscale
  • .jpg file format

Zip all files into one zip file. Make sure to rotate your images so they are easy to read, and enhance the levels/contrast if necessary.

File names for your images should be in this format: xxx_wireframe_01.jpg (replace xx with your Drexel ID, update file numbers as needed)
